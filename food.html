The Influence of Urbanization on Air Quality in Dhaka, Bangladesh

  1. Introduction: 

Urbanization is happening all over the world, and it has both good and bad effects on the environment. One of the worst effects is that the air quality is getting worse, which poses serious health risks to people who live in cities. Bangladesh's capital city, Dhaka, is a great example of a city that is struggling with the effects of rapid urbanization on air quality.


Over 20 million people live in Dhaka, which is one of the cities in the world that is growing the fastest. In the last few decades, the city has grown very quickly, with a big rise in population, industrial activities, and car traffic. The city's air quality has gotten much worse because of how quickly it has grown. This makes Dhaka one of the most polluted cities in the world.


IQAir's 2022 World Air Quality Report says that Dhaka is always one of the cities with the worst air quality around the world. The air quality in the city is bad because it has a lot of particulate matter (PM2.5), which is very bad for people's health. High levels of PM2.5 have been linked to a number of health problems, such as lung diseases, heart diseases, and death before its time.


The main reason why the air quality in Dhaka is getting worse is because the city is growing. These include more pollution from cars and factories, more pollution from construction, and the burning of solid waste and biomass. The rapid growth and development of the city has also led to the loss of green spaces, which help clean the air by soaking up pollution.


Even though things are bad, efforts have been made to improve the air quality in the city. Policies have been put in place to limit emissions from vehicles and encourage the use of cleaner fuels. But these steps haven't had much of an effect, and Dhaka still has a big problem with air pollution.


The goal of this research proposal is to find out how urbanization affects the quality of the air in Dhaka, Bangladesh. The study will give a full picture of the specific things that contribute to the city's bad air quality. This will help with urban planning and pollution control in the future.

  1. Rationale: 

The reason for doing this study is that it could help improve public health, the economy, and urban planning in Dhaka. By looking at how urbanization affects air quality, this research can help come up with good ways to deal with air pollution in areas that are growing quickly.

Air pollution is a worldwide environmental problem that puts people's health at risk and costs a lot of money. It is especially bad in cities that are growing quickly, where industrial activities, pollution from cars, and more people all contribute to worsening air quality. The capital city of Bangladesh, Dhaka, is a good example of a city like this. Even though it is a very important issue, no one has really looked into how urbanization affects the air quality in Dhaka. This study aims to fill in this gap in knowledge and give a full picture of how urbanization affects the air quality in Dhaka.


This study has many reasons for being done. First of all, air pollution is a big problem for public health. The World Health Organization says that air pollution causes several million early deaths around the world every year. The health effects are especially bad in Dhaka, where air pollution levels often go above international safety standards. When you know what makes the air in Dhaka bad, you can take steps to reduce the health risks caused by air pollution.


Second, air pollution has big effects on the economy. It can make health care costs go up, make workers sicker and less productive, and lower crop yields because the soil and water are dirty. This study can help find ways to reduce the economic costs of Dhaka's air pollution by looking at how urbanization affects air quality.


Third, this study is important and timely because Dhaka is becoming more urban very quickly. The city's population is expected to keep growing over the next few decades, and as it does, problems like air pollution that come with living in a city are likely to get worse. Policymakers, urban planners, and environmentalists who are trying to deal with the effects of urbanization and improve the air quality in Dhaka can learn a lot from this research.


Lastly, there have been studies on air pollution in Dhaka, but there hasn't been much research on how urbanization affects air quality. This study will add to what is known about this subject and lay the groundwork for future research.


  1. Objective: 

The main goal of this study is to find out how urbanization affects the air quality in Dhaka, Bangladesh, and to find out where most of the city's air pollution comes from.

In order to reach this big goal, the study will focus on a few specific goals:


  1. The first goal is to measure how dirty the air is in Dhaka, focusing on key pollutants like PM2.5 and PM10 particulate matter, NO2 nitrogen dioxide, and SO2 sulfur dioxide. This will require gathering and analyzing data from monitoring stations all over the city about the quality of the air.

  2. Find the Biggest Sources of Air Pollution: The second goal is to find the biggest sources of air pollution in Dhaka. To do this, we will have to look at how pollutants are made and where they are in the city. The study will look at both "point sources" of pollution (like factories and power plants) and "non-point sources" (like car traffic and home heating).

  3. Figure out how urbanization affects the air quality in Dhaka. The third goal is to figure out how urbanization affects the air quality in Dhaka. This will require looking at the trends in data about air quality and how they relate to signs of urbanization, such as population growth, changes in how land is used, and economic growth. The study will also look at how policies and actions in cities affect air quality.

  4. Look at the effects of air pollution on health and the economy. The fourth goal is to look at the effects of air pollution on health and the economy in Dhaka. This will require looking at health data (like hospital admissions and death rates) and economic data (like healthcare costs and lost productivity) in relation to levels of air pollution.

  5. Make suggestions for improving the air quality: The last goal is to make suggestions for improving the air quality in Dhaka. This will require putting together a summary of the study's results to help make plans for controlling air pollution and planning cities. The recommendations will be made for policymakers, urban planners, and environmentalists, among other groups.


By reaching these goals, the study hopes to give a full picture of how urbanization affects the quality of the air in Dhaka, Bangladesh. The results will help with plans to clean up the city's air and improve people's health.


    4. Research Questions: 

The following research questions will be the focus of this study:


  1. How bad is the air pollution in Dhaka right now? 


  1. What are the main things that make the air in Dhaka dirty? 


  1. How has the growth of Dhaka's cities changed the air quality there? 


  1. What are the patterns of air pollution in Dhaka in terms of space and time? 


  1. What are the effects of Dhaka's dirty air on people's health? 


  1. What are the financial costs of Dhaka's dirty air? 


  1. How well have past and current policies in Dhaka worked to clean up the air? 


  1. What can be done to improve the quality of the air in Dhaka? Give us suggestions on how to control air pollution and plan cities.


These research questions are meant to give a full picture of how urbanization affects the quality of the air in Dhaka, Bangladesh. How to deal with air pollution and improve public health in the city will depend on the answers to these questions.


    5. Literature Review:

Several studies have explored the impact of urbanization on air quality in Dhaka. For instance, a study by Islam et al. (2020) found that lockdown measures during the COVID-19 pandemic significantly reduced air pollution due to reduced vehicular and industrial emissions. Another study by Begum & Hopke (2017) found that despite increasing economic activity and sources of pollution, the air quality in Dhaka has remained stable over the past decade due to policy implementations.

A study by Pavel et al. (2021) conducted a long-term analysis of air quality in Dhaka from 2003 to 2019. The study found that the levels of air pollution in Dhaka have been increasing significantly over the years. The study also identified vehicular emissions, road/soil dust, biomass burning, and industrial emissions as the main sources of air pollution in the city.

In a study by Begum & Hopke (2017), the authors used a combination of principal component analysis (PCA) and positive matrix factorization (PMF) to identify the main sources of air pollution in Dhaka. The study found that vehicular emissions, road/soil dust, biomass burning, and industrial emissions were the main contributors to air pollution in the city.

A study by Ferdous et al. (2020) explored the impact of secondhand smoke on indoor air quality in Dhaka. The study found that homes where smoking was permitted had significantly higher levels of particulate matter (PM2.5) compared to smoke-free homes.

Finally, a study by Pavel et al. (2014) investigated the household air quality risk factors associated with childhood pneumonia in urban Dhaka. The study found that crowding, a tin roof in the living space, low socioeconomic status, and male sex of the child were risk factors for pneumonia. The living space in case households was 28% less likely than in control households to be cross-ventilated.

These studies provide valuable insights into the impact of urbanization on air quality in Dhaka. However, there is still a need for further research to understand the specific factors contributing to air pollution in the city and to develop effective strategies for air pollution control.

    6. Method of Analysis: 

This study will use a mix of methods, including a quantitative analysis of data about air quality and qualitative interviews with local stakeholders. Statistical analysis will be used to look for trends and correlations with indicators of urbanization. The data will come from monitoring stations all over Dhaka. Interviews with local stakeholders will give information about how people live and what they think the effects of urbanization on air quality are.


    7. Results:

We want to make suggestions for improving the air quality in Dhaka based on what we find. These could include policy suggestions for reducing emissions, urban planning strategies to keep and grow green spaces, and public health interventions to lessen the effects of air pollution on people's health. We expect to find a strong link between signs of urbanization (like population growth and changes in land use) and levels of air pollution. This would show that urbanization has a real effect on the air quality in Dhaka. We also hope to find out how Dhaka's air pollution affects health and the economy.


Please keep in mind that these are the expected results based on what is already known and the goals of the study. The real results may be different depending on the data collected and what was going on in Dhaka at the time of the study.


    8. The Work Plan and Budget:

The research should take about six months to finish, and the tentative schedule is as follows:


  1. Month 1: Starting the project and reviewing the literature: The first month will be spent refining the research plan, doing a full review of the literature, and figuring out where the data will come from.

  2. Month 2: Collecting Data: During the second month, data about the air quality will be collected from different monitoring stations all over Dhaka. This will also involve collecting important data on urbanization, health, and the economy.

  3. Months 3 and 4: Data Analysis: The collected data will be looked at in the third and fourth months. This will require both quantitative and qualitative analysis of policy documents and interviews with stakeholders to find trends and connections.

  4. Month 5: Interpreting Results and Writing Report: During the fifth month, the results of the data analysis will be interpreted and the research report will be written. This includes making suggestions for how to improve the air quality in Dhaka.

  5. Month 6: Review and Finalization: The last month will be spent looking over the research report, making any changes that need to be made, and getting the report ready to send or publish.


The cost of this research is expected to be 9,50,000 BDT. Among these are:


  • Data Collection (2,00,000 BDT): This covers the costs of getting access to data about air quality, buying relevant datasets, and interviewing stakeholders.

  • Data Analysis (2,50,000 BDT): This includes the costs of statistical software, data visualization tools, and possible consulting fees for complex data analysis.

  • Personnel (4,00,000 BDT): This covers the salaries or stipends for the research team, which includes the principal investigator, research assistants, and any consultants.

  • Administrative and Other Costs (1,00,000 BDT): This includes costs for writing reports, communicating, traveling to meet with stakeholders, and any other costs that come up.


Please keep in mind that this is a preliminary budget that may need to be changed depending on the specifics of the research and what is needed.



    9. Conclusion:

The goal of the proposed research is to get a full picture of how urbanization affects the air quality in Dhaka, Bangladesh. Given how fast the city is growing and the problems that come with it, this study is both timely and important. By looking into how urbanization affects air quality, the research will help us learn more about the specific factors that cause air pollution in Dhaka. This information is important for making plans to deal with air pollution and improve public health in the city.


The results of the study will affect a wide range of people, such as policymakers, urban planners, environmentalists, and public health workers. By finding out what the main sources of air pollution are and how they are linked to urbanization, the research can help policymakers and people working to improve air quality make decisions and take action. Also, by putting a number on the health and economic effects of air pollution, the study can show how important it is to solve this problem and help reduce the health risks that come with bad air quality.


In the end, this research is an important step toward understanding and dealing with the problems caused by air pollution in a world where cities are growing quickly. The results will add to what is known about this topic and lay the groundwork for future research and action to improve the air quality in Dhaka and other cities that are growing quickly around the world.
